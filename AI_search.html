AI Search Techniques

Introduction

This section introduces the concept of search techniques in AI.

Adversarial Search

Adversarial search involves scenarios where agents are competing against each other, like in games. The most common example is the Minimax algorithm used in two-player games.

Informed Search

Informed search, also known as heuristic search, involves using problem-specific knowledge or heuristics to find solutions more efficiently than uninformed search methods. Algorithms like A* and Greedy Search fall under this category.

Uninformed Search

Uninformed search, or blind search, is the method of searching without any domain-specific knowledge. Algorithms like Breadth-First Search and Depth-First Search are examples of uninformed search.
